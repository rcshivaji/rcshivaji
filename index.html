Education

I divide my school education into two parts, in the first part there are many changes, this was till 7th class and in the next part I just stayed in one school. I completed my kindergarten in Kolkata and then moved onto Burdwan, West Bengal, where I got enrolled into the prestigious St.Xaviers School. I did my 1st standard there. Following that I spent the following three years in Dehradun, here I studied in Brightlands School. The following two years that is in 5th and 6th class I spent in Jaipur in Subodh Public School. My 7th class was divided into two parts, the first half I spent in Meerut in KL International School and then I moved onto Agra where I enrolled into St.Conrad's Inter College. St.Conrad's is the school that shaped me into what I am today, the teachers were very good, noteworthy mentioning Mrs Meena Chawla ma'am and Mrs Rupali Gautam ma'am. I spent the following six years in Agra, completing my board exams(10th and 12th) from there. Thanks to my supporting teachers I performed really well in both of my board exams, 94.4% in 10th and 93.25% in 12th. Another fact which is worth mentioning is the fact that I scored 99 in 10th board exams and 100 in 12th board exams, the latter made me think about my future choice that was Computer Science considering the fact that earlier I wanted to become a Civil Engineer. Another thumbs up to Umesh Tinna Sir whose coaching classes I took in 10th and 12th for programming and his effects were cery clear.

Following my school life which ended in 2017, I enrolled into Manipal University Jaipur to pursue B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering. If I speak truthfully, personality wise I changed a lot throughout the years in college, from waking up at 7:30 in the morning and going to classes 20 minutes before they started to waking up at 8:45 and reaching class by 9. In my school life hardly had I ever stayed awake past 12 but here it just became a new normal, be it studying a day before exams, playing FIFA/CSGO or watching Football matches with friends while ordering food from the restaurants that stayed open till 2. Also I had some of the best colleagues and friends who made my college life all the more memorable. Despite these things, it was not just a cake walk, I had some difficult times as well, academic as well as family related. But together with all the ups and downs I can truly say that college life turned me into a more experienced and self dependent and self confident person.
